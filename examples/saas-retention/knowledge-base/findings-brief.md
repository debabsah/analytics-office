## Findings
Each: observation -> implication -> action -> watch-for. Every claim carries its status `[status : source]`.

1. **Net Revenue Retention is 108% this quarter, up from 102% a year ago** `[Directional-only : kpi-contract v1.0, not yet reconciled to Finance]`
   - Implication: on the contracted definition the existing base is growing on its own, which would favor funding growth over a dedicated onboarding team.
   - Action: hold the recommendation until the Finance reconciliation closes (open item 1). Present the 108% as directional, not as a certified figure.
   - Watch-for: "Finance says total revenue grew about 2%, you say retention is 108%, which is wrong?" This is the question that cracked the rehearsal.

2. **Gross revenue churn improved to 4.2% monthly** `[Supported : kpi-contract v1.0]`
   - Implication: cancellation and contraction pressure is easing, consistent with the NRR direction.
   - Action: present it beside NRR, never alone; gross churn falling while NRR is flat would mean expansion is slowing.
   - Watch-for: a board member reading the two together and asking what expansion is doing.

3. **The prior board churn number came from a view that measured the wrong thing** `[Supported : query-review.md, 4 Blocking findings]`
   - Implication: earlier slides counted logos, not revenue, and understated the bleed; the number changing is a correction, not a moved goalpost.
   - Action: state plainly that the metric was corrected and re-based on billing MRR per the locked contract.
   - Watch-for: "Did you move the goalposts since last year?" (last year's 102% was recomputed on the same basis.)

4. **The decision actually turns on early-life (first-90-day) churn by cohort, which is not built** `[Open - needs decision : requirements-brief.md, open-questions.md]`
   - Implication: blended NRR hides early-life behavior; "invest in growth, not onboarding" is unsupported without this cut.
   - Action: build the cohort cut before the recommendation is made; do not assert the recommendation in the meantime.
   - Watch-for: "What is retention in the first 90 days?" The honest answer today is that we do not have it.
